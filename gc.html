The GameCube and Wii, are special in that their games have largely UNIQUE aspect ratios. The WiiU doesn't have this issue, and the N64 doesn't either, but the GameCube and Wii do, for whatever reason. The semi-reason behind this, is that since old TVs used analogue signals, they would have an effect called overscan occur, where basically the TV would take the signal from a game console, and interpret it and convert it to TV signals, and along the way, lose some of the signal. This causes game footage from the corners and borders of a game to be iffy at best for if they were to be visible, as most TVs have different overscan interpretations.

Illustrated here is what the GameCube actually outputs, in green, and what a TV might see, in red. Since older TVs are 4:3 they'll show a 4:3 image, but for some reason the game itself isn't. Other game consoles output a standard 4:3 and there doesn't have to be any TV magic going on, but the GameCube is an exception in that matter.

For whatever reason, the GameCube takes this issue, and makes a giant mess out of it, where basically, to compensate for TVs chopping off some parts of the signal, the GameCube doesn't define a set size for its signal like most consoles, and only requires games shoot out 480 lines of video. I've compiled a comprehensive, yet growing list of GameCube and Wii aspect/pixel ratios so people can know how to display their games. Some people have their capture cards set up to simply treat everything as 4:3, which doesn't work for these two consoles, and some setups have some weird aspect setups, where its a semi stretched 4:3 game feed going out, it's basically a mess, and so with this hopefully people can know how to display their games correctly if they so care. Some games are fine to display in 4:3, it isn't too far from the source ratio, but some games like Melee are so far from 4:3, that you really lose noticable quality displaying them in that ratio. All games in here will be defined by x480, where each game has 480 vertical lines, and the horizontal section is what is up for grabs. For reference, 4:3 is 640x480.
