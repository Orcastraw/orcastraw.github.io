The SNES and NES both output 4:3 to a TV, and the aspect ratio used in Nintendo's Wii and Wii U Virtual Console for SNES/NES is 4:3 also. However the consoles actually output 8:7 natively, and then stretch that signal to 4:3. There's no "right" way to project their image, no consensus on which is better, 4:3 is just more traditional and used more. The newly released SNES VC for the New Nintendo 3DS even has the option to choose between 8:7 or 4:3 display for your games, it really is up to you!

There's decent reason to use 8:7 however. Several games look better in 8:7, their native ratio, such as Yoshi's Island, or Super Metroid. Furthermore, you'll see more shape preservation, as squares, previously impossible on a SNES, form with the tiles in Metroid, and for Yoshi's, the ovals become cute cirlces.

Another great reason to use 8:7 instead of defaulting to 4:3 like most people do... It's used in remakes! Super Mario World on the GBA, Link To The Past on the GBA, and Yoshi's Island on the GBA all use the graphics from a 8:7 interpretation of the SNES originals.

Furthermore, the recently released Mario Maker uses sprite rips from 8:7 Super Mario World as well. This image I made shows the 4 variations of SMW, and shows the direct sprite rips from all of them, with me stretching the 4:3 mario myself. The only version with the big nosed Mario is 4:3 SMW, and the rest are skinny and consistent.

Super Metroid doesn't have a re-release to compare, but it does have several later entries in the series, all of which has a CIRCULAR morph ball! 4:3 Super Metroid is the only entry with an ovular morph ball, as this infographic points out.

Some games are designed with the stretching in mind, and should not be toyed with putting 8:7 at all, such as Chrono Trigger. In that game, opposite from most SNES games, to get pefect shapes you actually need to be in 4:3, or else you'll get messy ovals.

Overall, 8:7 can be seen as preferable for most games, but people have been using 4:3 for years, including Nintendo's VC and your old TV you used to display your SNES on. Either is fine. :^)
